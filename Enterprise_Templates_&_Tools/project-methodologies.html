Compare Methodologies

Different projects have different needs. A methodology that works for a highly predictable construction project (like Waterfall) will not work for a fast-moving software startup (like Agile/Scrum). This section helps you compare these approaches at a glance.

Agile (Scrum/Kanban)

Core Principle: Iterative and incremental progress. Embraces change and customer feedback.

Best For: Projects with unclear or evolving requirements, software development, R&D, and startups.

Waterfall

Core Principle: Linear and sequential. Each phase must be completed before the next begins.

Best For: Projects with fixed, clear requirements, a stable scope, and predictable outcomes, like construction or manufacturing.

PRINCE2

Core Principle: Process-driven. Focuses on business justification, defined roles, and product-based planning.

Best For: Large, formal, or enterprise-level projects that require significant oversight, control, and documentation.
